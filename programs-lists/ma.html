Course Description

The Master of Arts Theological Studies is a three-contact (June-July) degree Postgraduate programme. It is a ministry-oriented degree designed to help ministers develop theological reflection on ministerial practice. It places emphasis on practical theology, applying cutting-edge academic study and research to contemporary ministry. At the end of the programme, the students will be:

  • equipped with skills, strategies, and theology for solving complex and challenging problems in pluralistic and post-Christendom contexts.
  • helped to be spiritually renewed and to grow in their understanding and interpretation of the church’s ministry biblically, theologically, historically, ethically and practically.
  • prepared to articulate continued growth in professional development, spiritual maturity, and ministerial identity.

Requirements

The Master of Arts in Theological Studies is a three-contact sessions Part-time programme. The maximum number of contact sessions for the programme is six contact sessions.

  • Candidates for the Master of Arts in Theological Studies programme must hold a B.Th. or its equivalent from institutions approved by the BTSK with a minimum of second class lower performance.
  • Candidates are also expected to have at least one year post-qualification experience.
  • It is expected that the candidate had the minimum requirement at ordinary levels as indicated for bachelor degree programmes.
  • It is expected that the candidate had the minimum requirement as indicated in the general requirements.
