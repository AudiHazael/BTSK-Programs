Course Description

The Bachelor in Chaplaincy is a four-year degree programme incorporating courses in biblical studies, theological studies, religious educational studies, practical theological studies and chaplaincy courses. The purpose of the programme is to train and prepare knowledgeable and skilled institutional chaplains in the army, hospitals, prisons, gospel rescue missions, police service, and schools. At the end of the completion of the study, students should be able demonstrate:

  • practical skills on how to start, monitor, and evaluate chaplaincy programme.
  • leadership and administrative skills related to chaplaincy.
  • practical skills of taking care people who are hurting, lonely, in crisis or destitute outside the walls of the church
  • The skill to interpret the biblical writings in the Greek or Hebrew languages, using critical exegetical tools.
  • A basic knowledge of the key movements and figures, and their significance in the history of Christianity.
  • A basic knowledge of the contents and methods of Christian theology.
  • necessary skills for ministry within a variety of traditions, ministry settings, and cross-cultural contexts.

Requirements

These bachelor programs are a minimum of four and maximum of six years for the regular students; and, a minimum of eight and maximum of twelve contact sessions for the part-time student. An exception applies to students who have credited courses from the previous course of study depending on the hours credited for them. Candidates not able to complete their studies within the stipulated time will be withdrawn from the programme. Candidates seeking admission to the four-year regular programme or the eight-contact session Part-time programme must have the general entry requirements plus the following:

  • (Either) WASC, NECO or GCE with a minimum of five relevant credits, which must include English Language, in not more than two sittings;
  • In addition to “a” above NCE with five credits in relevant subjects including English Language;
  • (Or) Grade II with five credits/merits including English Language
  • Candidates who meet the above requirements and have completed a diploma programme in Theology or Christian Religious Education in a school recognized by the seminary may be given credit for relevant courses passed at a level of “B” or above to a maximum of seventy credit hours. An official transcript must be received directly from the previous institution (registered mail is suggested) before placement credit can be considered.
  • Candidates applying for B. Th. part-time programme must have had previous theological training in addition to the above requirement.
  • It is expected that the candidate had the minimum requirement as indicated in the general requirements.

The Baptist Theological Seminary Kaduna is an academic institution owned by the Nigerian Baptist Convention. The primary purpose of the Seminary is to provide sound theological education and pastoral training for those aspects of the Christian Ministry.
