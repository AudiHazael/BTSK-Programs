Programme Description

Masters of Theology level of study is designed to empower the student by providing in-depth reflection on selected study fields of their choices. It is a research degree that combines spiritual and intellectual enquiries. Specific objectives of the Masters of Theology degrees are provided below according to the areas of specialization.

Programme Specializations

M. Th. Old Testament

At the end of the course of study in M. Th. Old Testament, the student should be able to:

  • Demonstrate the ability to critically evaluate and exegete the text of the Old Testament in its original languages.
  • Demonstrate the ability to evaluate the linguistic characteristics and historical questions related to the Old Testament.
  • Demonstrate the ability to survey and assess the theology of the Old Testament in Nigerian context.
  • Demonstrate analytical skills needed to do research in biblical and theological fields.

M. Th. New Testament

At the end of the course of study in M. Th. New Testament, the student should be able to:

  • Demonstrate the ability to critically evaluate and exegete the text of the New Testament in its original language.
  • Demonstrate the ability to evaluate the linguistic characteristics and historical questions related to the New Testament.
  • Demonstrate the ability to survey and assess the theology of the New Testament in Nigerian context.
  • Demonstrate analytical skills needed to do research in biblical and theological fields.

M. Th. Systematic Theology

At the end of the course of study in M.Th. Systematic Theology, the student should be able to:

  • Apply biblical and theological principles to the life and ministry of the Church.
  • Understand and justify major theological themes from Biblical, systematic and contemporary perspectives.
  • Evaluate and proffer solution to theological challenges facing the Church today.
  • Exposed to the tapestry of global theological concerns.

M.Th. Missions

At the end of the course of study in M.Th. Missions, the student should be able to:

  • Articulate the biblical basis for missions in its various arms such as evangelism, church planting, discipleship and church growth.
  • Demonstrate adequate knowledge of the theories and practice of missions and be able to develop effective strategies for mission and evangelism in contemporary times.
  • Demonstrate adequate skills in reaching out to Muslims and Traditional Religious groups and other inter-faith contexts.
  • Integrate discipleship as the key to effective missions and preserving missionary consciousness of the Church.

M.Th. Pastoral Care and Counselling

At the end of the course of study in M.Th. Pastoral Care and Counselling, the student should be able to:

  • Develop a personal and pastoral technique of counselling.
  • Apply pastoral theology and scriptures to human problems.
  • Demonstrate analytical skills needed to do research in pastoral care and Counselling.

M.Th. Christian Philosophy

At the end of the course of study in M.Th. Christian Philosophy, the student should be able to:

  • Demonstrate an understanding of philosophical principles and their relationship to religious expressions in general.
  • Develop skill in the use of philosophical tools in affirming the value and setting the limits of philosophy is dealing with faith issues.
  • Affirm the value of philosophy in understanding worldviews and relating Christian truth to the total person.

M.Th. Christian Ethics

At the end of the course of study in M.Th. Christian Ethics, the student should be able to:

  • Affirm the Bible as the foundation of Christian ethics.
  • Identify and apply biblical principles to contexts in Church and society.
  • Become familiar with ethical situation of the world from the biblical period to the present.
  • Able to provide biblical response to ethical implications on religious, societal, material and environmental contexts.

M. Th. Church History

At the end of the course of study in M. Th. Church History, the student should be able to:

  • Identify the major time periods in the history of the church and associate key events with these periods.
  • Understand and appreciate significant historical issues in the life of the church from the earliest moments of its history to the present.
  • Recognize important personalities in the church’s long history and the role they played in shaping the church.
  • Affirm the presence of the Holy Spirit in the broad sweep of events from the time of Jesus Christ through the reformation to the present.
  • Understand and appreciate the church’s struggles with forces from within and from without and how doctrines developed and became part of the church’s spiritual heritage.
  • Demonstrate an adequate knowledge of the emergence and development of the Baptist denomination from the seventeenth century to date.
  • Demonstrate adequate understanding of how the history of the church in Africa is to be written or told.
  • Demonstrate adequate understanding of how the history of the church in Africa is to be written or told.
  • Understand how to offer an objective, reasonable, and unbiased interpretation of the sources, based on the laws of critical reasoning.
  • Demonstrate an adequate understanding and appreciation of the origin, contributions of, and Challenges faced by the church in Africa before and after the advent of missionary Christianity.

MTh Christian Preaching

Objectives of the discipline are as follows:

  • To lead students to an understanding and evaluation of the historical development of Christian preaching.
  • To expose students to the major concept of biblical preaching.
  • To equip students with the necessary skills to do effective expository preaching based on a solid understanding of key doctrinal issues and sound homiletical principles.
  • To expose students to the concept of evangelistic preaching, in order to acquire a basic and fundamental knowledge of how to lead people to faith in Christ Jesus.
  • To expose students to the elements and principles of the preparation and delivery of biblical messages using a variety of homiletical techniques and designs.

Requirements

The Masters of Theology envisaged by the BTSK is a two-year (four semesters) fully residential study. It will involve lectures, seminars, colloquia and a research thesis, and the maximum number of years shall be four within which the student must complete his or her program of study. Failure to meet up the maximum number of years shall lead to termination of study.

  • Candidates for the M.Th. programme must hold a B.Th. or its equivalent from institutions approved by the BTSK with a minimum of second class lower performance
  • Candidates are also expected to have at least one year post-qualification experience.
  • It is expected that the candidate had the minimum requirement as indicated in the general requirements.

The Baptist Theological Seminary Kaduna is an academic institution owned by the Nigerian Baptist Convention. The primary purpose of the Seminary is to provide sound theological education and pastoral training for those aspects of the Christian Ministry.
