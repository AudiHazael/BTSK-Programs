Course Description

This Master of Divinity is a two-year degree Postgraduate programme. It is a specialized programmed designed to prepare students who have completed required Postgraduate Diploma or equivalent degrees for further studies in the areas of Biblical, Systematic Theology, Christian Missions, Church History and Practical Theology studies. By the end of the M.Div specialized programme, the students should be to:

  • Empower the student by providing in-depth reflection on selected study fields of their choices.
  • Demonstrate the professional skills needed in the various related ministries of teaching, preaching, evangelism, leadership, and Christian worship.
  • Provide opportunities to develop necessary skills for ministry within a variety of traditions, ministry settings, and cross-cultural contexts.
  • Enable students to develop and articulate a theology and theory of ministry that is relevant to their backgrounds and callings.
  • Demonstrate knowledge and skills of theological education to work and minister in an interfaith setting among adherents of Islam and African Traditional Religion.

Course Specialization

M.Div Biblical Studies

The Master of Divinity (Biblical Studies) is a specialized programme for equipping candidates with principles and approaches to Old and New Testament studies, and for ensuring competence in exegesis, exposition and biblical interpretation. At the end of the course of study, the student should be able to:

  • Demonstrate the ability to critically evaluate and exegete the biblical text in its original languages.
  • Demonstrate the ability to survey and assess the theology of the Old and New Testaments in Nigerian context.
  • Demonstrate analytical skills needed to do research in biblical and theological fields.
  • Demonstrate the professional skills needed in the various related ministries of teaching, preaching, evangelism, leadership, and Christian worship.
  • Provide opportunities to develop necessary skills for ministry within a variety of traditions, ministry settings, and cross-cultural contexts.

MDiv Theology/Philosophy

The Master of Divinity (Theology/Philosophy) is a specialized programme for equipping candidates with principles and approaches to theological and philosophical studies. At the end of the course of study, the student should be able to:

  • Apply biblical and theological principles to the life and ministry of the Church.
  • Understand and justify major theological themes from Biblical, systematic and contemporary perspectives.
  • Demonstrate analytical skills needed to do research in biblical and theological fields.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of philosophical principles and their relationship to religious expressions in general.
  • Demonstrate the professional skills needed in the various related ministries of teaching, preaching, evangelism, leadership, and Christian worship.
  • Provide opportunities to develop necessary skills for ministry within a variety of traditions, ministry settings, and cross-cultural contexts.

MDiv Missions/Church History

The Master of Divinity (Missions and Church History) is a specialized programme for equipping candidates with principles and approaches to Christian Missions and historical studies. At the end of the course of study, the student should be able to:

  • Identify the major time periods in the history of the church and associate key events with these periods.
  • Understand and appreciate significant historical issues in the life of the church from the earliest moments of its history to the present.
  • Articulate the biblical basis for missions in its various arms such as evangelism, church planting, discipleship and church growth.
  • Demonstrate adequate knowledge of the theories and practice of missions and be able to develop effective strategies for mission and evangelism in contemporary times.
  • Demonstrate the professional skills needed in the various related ministries of teaching, preaching, evangelism, leadership, and Christian worship.
  • Provide opportunities to develop necessary skills for ministry within a variety of traditions, ministry settings, and cross-cultural contexts.

MDiv Practical Theology

The Master of Divinity (Practical Theology) is a specialized programme for equipping candidates with principles and approaches to ethics, counselling, preaching and pastoral care. At the end of the course of study, the student should be able to:

  • Develop a personal and pastoral technique of counselling.
  • Affirm the Bible as the foundation of Christian ethics.
  • Develop skills related to preaching sermons.
  • Demonstrate the professional skills needed in the various related ministries of teaching, preaching, evangelism, leadership, and Christian worship.
  • Provide opportunities to develop necessary skills for ministry within a variety of traditions, ministry settings, and cross-cultural contexts.

Requirements

The Master of Divinity is a three-year fully residential study. The maximum number of years of the programme is five years.

  • Candidates seeking admission into this programme must possess a minimum of a degree or its equivalent from a recognized institution of higher learning.
  • The applicant must have a minimum of second class lower classification (2.5) or better to be eligible.
  • Candidate without relevant first degree (especially Bachelor of Arts in CRS or equivalent) will be required to undertake one year Postgraduate Dipoma.
  • It is expected that the candidate had the minimum requirement as indicated in the general requirements.

The Baptist Theological Seminary Kaduna is an academic institution owned by the Nigerian Baptist Convention. The primary purpose of the Seminary is to provide sound theological education and pastoral training for those aspects of the Christian Ministry.
